# Shopping-trends-analysis
Retail shopping trends analysis using SQL and BI visualization

> Exploratory data analysis of 3,900 retail transactions using Python and SQL — uncovering customer behavior patterns and actionable business insights.
 
 
# 📌 Project Overview
 
This project analyzes a retail shopping dataset to understand customer purchasing behavior, identify revenue drivers, and generate data-backed business recommendations.
 
**Tools used:** SQL (SQLite · DBeaver), BI visualization (Tableau / Power BI)
 
**Dataset:** 3,900 transactions · 19 features · synthetic retail data

**Dashboard:** https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/anton.nikitiuk/viz/ShoppingTrendsAnalysisRetailCustomerInsights/Dashboard1#1

## 💡 Key Business Insights

**1. Clothing dominates revenue but has the lowest rating**
Clothing generates $104,264 (45% of total revenue) yet holds the lowest 
review rating (3.72/5) among all categories. High sales volume masks a 
potential quality or satisfaction issue worth investigating.

**2. The 56–70 age segment is the most valuable customer group**
Customers aged 56–70 generate $65,256 — nearly 2× more than the 18–25 
group ($34,630). This segment is likely underrepresented in marketing 
efforts despite being the highest-value cohort.

**3. The loyalty program is not working**
Subscribers (27% of customers) spend an average of $59.49 vs $59.87 
for non-subscribers. The subscription program shows no measurable impact 
on purchase value and needs a complete strategy overhaul.

**4. Discounts reduce average order value**
Customers who received a discount spent $59.28 on average vs $60.13 
without a discount. Discounts are distributed without an upsell strategy 
and are simply reducing margin.

**5. Seasonality is stable but Summer underperforms**
Revenue across seasons is relatively stable ($55K–$60K), but Summer 
consistently lags behind Fall by ~$4,241. A targeted Summer campaign 
could close this gap and add meaningful revenue.
